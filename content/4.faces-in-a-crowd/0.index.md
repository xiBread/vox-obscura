# Faces in a Crowd

::quotation
---
author: David Foster Wallace
source: Girl with Curious Hair
---

We all have our little solipsistic delusions, ghastly intuitions of utter singularity: that we are
the only one in the house who ever fills the ice-cube tray, who unloads the clean dishwasher, who
occasionally pees in the shower, whose eyelid twitches on first dates; that only we take casualness
terribly seriously; that only we fashion supplication into courtesy; that only we hear the whiny
pathos in a dog's yawn, the timeless sigh in the opening of the hermetically-sealed jar, the
splattered laugh in that frying egg, the minor-D lament in the vacuum's scream; that only we feel
the panic at sunset the rookie kindergartner feels at his mother's retreat. That only we love the
only-we. That only we need the only-we. Solipsism binds us together.... That we feel lonely in a
crowd; stop not to dwell on what's brought the crowd into being. That we are always, faces in a
crowd.
::
