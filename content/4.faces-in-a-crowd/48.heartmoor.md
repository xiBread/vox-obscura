::definition
*n.* the primal longing for a home village to return to, a place that no longer exists, if it ever
did; the fantasy of finding your way back home before nightfall, hustling to bring in the cattle
before the rains come; picturing a cluster of lanterns glowing on the edge of a tangled wood,
hearing the rattle and hiss of meals cooking over a communal fire, finding your place in a crowded
longhouse made of clay-packed thatch, where you'd sit and listen to the voices of four generations
layered into a canon, telling stories of a time when people could still melt into a collective
personality and weren't just floating around alone.

#details
From *heart* + *moor*, to tie a boat to an anchor. Pronounced "*hahrt*-moor."
::
